Patient Information on Glucocorticoid Therapy

Information May 26, 2025 Lali Nikoleishvili 6 min read

Information for patients receiving glucocorticoid (steroid) therapy

Glucocorticoids (steroid medications) are widely used in the treatment of many chronic conditions — inflammatory diseases, autoimmune disorders, allergic reactions, and respiratory pathologies. Despite their high effectiveness, long-term use requires careful physician monitoring and strict patient adherence to recommendations.

What to monitor during therapy

Regular check-ups are essential during glucocorticoid therapy. Our doctors recommend monitoring the following parameters:

  • Blood glucose levels — steroids increase insulin resistance and can cause steroid-induced diabetes.
  • Blood pressure — worsening or new-onset hypertension is common.
  • Bone density — risk of osteoporosis increases, particularly with therapy longer than 6 months.
  • Intraocular pressure — risk of glaucoma and cataracts increases.
  • Weight and fluid retention — rapid weight gain and edema may occur.

Nutrition recommendations

A balanced diet is important during therapy. Our dietitians advise:

  • Limit salt intake (under 1500 mg/day) to reduce blood pressure and swelling.
  • Increase calcium and vitamin D intake for bone health.
  • Avoid simple sugars and refined carbohydrates.
  • Increase protein intake to maintain muscle mass.
Important: never stop glucocorticoid therapy abruptly without your doctor's recommendation. Sudden withdrawal of steroids is dangerous and can cause adrenal insufficiency.

When to seek immediate medical attention

Immediate consultation is needed if you experience:

  1. Rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath, or chest pain
  2. Severe headache or visual disturbances
  3. Sudden spike in blood glucose
  4. Fever or signs of infection
  5. Severe abdominal pain or black stools

DIACOR's role in your treatment journey

Our multidisciplinary team — endocrinologists, cardiologists, pulmonologists, and ophthalmologists — works together under one roof to ensure every aspect of therapy stays under control. Regular visits allow us to detect and manage any complications early.

Please note: this article is for general information only and does not replace your doctor's consultation. Any change in treatment must be discussed with your treating physician.
